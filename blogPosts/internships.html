Resumes, Internsips and Cheat Sheets

Over the course of the past 9 months, I have spent a good chunk of my time preparing and applying to internships in the software enginering field. I have had my ups (nailed it!) and downs (blew it?!) throughout the process and learned some lessons that seem to be more or less universal.

Process

  • Backdoor: use referrals as much as possible (your 1x and 2x social circle).
  • Third door: prove that you can solve a problem faced by the company you are applying for.
  • Golden Hour: applications submitted within 2-4 days after a position is posted have the highest chance of getting an interview.
    • Apply consistently (vs 100 apps in Oct || 7 apps in Nov).
  • Numbers Game: use quickapply.io || huntr.co || or any other similar service.

Resume

  • Accomplishments Formula: use accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z].
  • Pareto Lens: write (ten) versions of a bullet point and pick one.
  • Missing Gems: don't miss impressive things you have done in the past
  • ATM: in most cases, you were/are/will be rejected by a machine
    • Make you resume machine parseable
    • Use keywords often
    • Use websites like resumeworded.com
